For this project, I served as a design consultant for Volu, a startup based in Syracuse, NY. I conducted design research, testings, and ideation to redesign the user experience and interface of Volu, addressing its shortcomings and the discovered design opportunities from the research.

UX Issues

Volu’s main product is a mobile application that allows users to sign up for volunteering events with a minimal onboarding process. Users only need to search, input names, emails, and phone numbers to register for events.

Besides some obvious UI issues, Volu’s emphasis on minimal onboarding process misses out a great opportunity for them to understand its user-base and establish a stronger relationship between volunteers, the communities, and Volu.

Competitive Analysis

There are several web platforms and mobile applications that share the same value offerings as Volu. One apparent difference is that their onboarding processes are often dull and lengthy. On the other hand, they offer more varieties of opportunities and provide event recommendations to their users.

    Advantages:
  • Sufficient understanding of their users
  • Huge varieties of opportunities offerings

    • Disadvantages:
  • Lengthy on-boarding process
  • Complex search and filter options

Interviews

The majority of the interviewees responded that they don’t usually volunteer because they didn’t have time. But they later revealed they viewed the process of looking for volunteering opportunities as “work”.

Some responded they would be more willing to volunteer if they are joined by their friends or family. In fact some responded that their latest volunteer experiences were invited by their friends or family.

    Findings
  • Volunteering events searching process is often viewed as work.
  • Volunteering should not be an isolated experience but a gateway for a meaningful relationship with the community.
  • Social factor is a big influencer for volunteering motivation and commitment.

Literature Review

From the article by Ram Cnann and Robin Goldberg-Glen, published by American Psychology Association, he points out there 6 distinctive motivations of volunteering. He also states that typical volunteers usually have 2 or more motivations, and tailored message about their motivations can greatly increase their chance of volunteering again.

Service Map

Volu lacks certain essential interactions between stakeholders to assist and encourage users to build a relationship with the community. With minimal understanding and interaction with their users, they are unable to motivate users to commit to an event or recommend users further opportunities.

There’s a huge communication gap between non-profit organizations and the users. And Volu doesn’t enable users to share the event information with others on social media and other platforms.

INSIGHTS

Searching for volunteer events is considered “work”
For people who seldom volunteers, some view the process of looking for volunteering opportunities as dull and tiring.

Commitment is the key to create successful volunteering events
Volunteering events often get canceled due to low attendance. And user often feel anxious and nervous before the events.

Volunteering is all about building relationships
 The holistic goal of volunteering is building a strong, and beneficial relationship between the volunteers and the communities around them.

Habit-Forming System

The goal of the gamification system is to help the user build a habit of volunteering. This system aims to assist the users to create prolonged, and beneficial relationships with the organizations.

Understanding of both the internal and external triggers and rewards can be utilized to encourage users to achieve the said goal.

Experience

Started with rough sketch, I continuously test the prototype with users as I progress to diagnose the pain points, and success of the experience. The flow of event signing up and creating profiles are switched to create a more encouraging experience for users to create profiles.Onboarding process is simplified down to digestible bits of information input on each page to encourage of user registration.

Sign Up

As user invests time to find an event and sign up, gamification system acknowledges the efforts and encourage them to go through the optional step of creating a profile. With more in-depth information of the users, the system gets a better understanding of them.

In the profile creation process, the system would gather users' skills, interest, and motivations to better recommend them other opportunities, and create follow up email content that tailored to their motivations of volunteering.

Gamification

The users would receive trophies for various achievements such as volunteering with the same organization for three times, volunteer once a month for three months in a row, and others to encourage them to form a relationship with the organizations, and a habit of volunteering.

Users are able to add and invite their friends to have a friendly competition with them to compete for points, trophies, and levels, to see who put forth the most effort in volunteering,

Daily Match

To make the volunteering search process less like work, the daily match feature is introduced for the user to find opportunities through a entertaining game.

There would be 10 rounds of binary selection. The user would select one card that they prefer. Through the process of deduction, the system would present the user the events that match the preference the most.
