VOLU

Volu connects volunteers with opportunities.

As a design consultant for Volu, a startup based in Syracuse, NY, I conducted design research, testing, and ideation to discover design opportunities and improve its shortcomings.

UX Issues

Users can sign up for volunteering events on Volu after a minimal onboarding process. Users only need to search for events and input necessary personal information to register for events.

Volu’s emphasis on the minimal onboarding process fails to establish a relationship with volunteers and communities because they know nothing about their users.

Competitive Analysis

Competitors’ onboarding processes are often dull and lengthy, but they can use users’ information to provide recommendations.

Interviews

People often overlook the holistic goal of volunteering. Volunteering is about building a relationship with communities and is not just an academic requirement.

Literature Review

He states that typical volunteers usually have more than one motivation. Additionally, tailored messages based on their motivations can successfully encourage repeated volunteering.

Service Map

With minimal understanding and interaction with users, Volu cannot motivate users to volunteer or provide recommendations.

A considerable communication gap exists between volunteer organizations and users. Furthermore, users cannot share event information on social media to invite others.

Insights

Searching for Volunteer Events Feels Like “Work”

Some users feel the process of looking for volunteering opportunities is dull and tiring.

Infrequent Volunteers Can Be Motivated to Volunteer

Yet, Volu can't understand its users with its minimal onboarding process.

Volunteering is About Building Relationships

The holistic goal of volunteering is building a healthy and beneficial relationship between volunteers and their communities.

Habit-Forming System

The system can help users build a habit of volunteering and establish a long-term relationship with organizations.

Understanding both the internal and external triggers and rewards can encourage users to achieve goals.

Experience Design

I tested the prototype to understand its pain points and successes. I also switched the steps of event sign-up and profile creation to entice users to create profiles.

The onboarding experience is broken into several pages to avoid being daunting. The gamification system makes searching for events less tiring and dull.

Interface Design

The event sign-up flow and profile creation flow are separated to avoid confusion. Intentional word choices and icons improve usability.

The interface is simplified to better explain the gamification system and ways to gain achievements.

Sign Up

As users invest time in searching and signing up for events, the gamification system awards the action and encourages users to create profiles.

By understanding users' skills, interests, and motivations, the app will recommend other opportunities and create personalized follow-up emails to users.

Daily Match

The daily match feature helps users find opportunities through a short game, making the process of looking for volunteering events seem less like work.

The user would select one card they prefer. After ten rounds, the system presents the events that match their preferences.

Gamification

Users would receive trophies for various achievements, such as:"Volunteering with the same organization on three different occasions" and "Volunteering once a month for three months in a row".

Users can add and invite their friends to compete to see who puts forth the most effort in volunteering.
