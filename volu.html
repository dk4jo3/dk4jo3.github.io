VOLU

Volu connects volunteers with volunteering opportunities.

As a design consultant for Volu, a startup based in Syracuse, NY, I conducted design research, testing, and ideation to discover design opportunities and improve its shortcomings.

UX Issues

Volu boasted how easy it is for users to find volunteering events with the app. Users can sign up for events after a minimal onboarding process; they only need to search for events and input essential personal information to register.

I downloaded the app and tested it with people around me. One reaction that stuck with me is what one student said after signing up in under one minute.

That’s it? I am not even sure if that [event registration] went through.


I began to question if Volu’s minimal onboarding process was beneficial after that. I formulated a research plan to validate my hypothesis.

Hypothesis

I believed Volu’s minimal onboarding process is too fast and too minimal to establish trust and ongoing relationships with its users.

User Research

Competitive Analysis

I checked out other volunteering platforms’ onboarding process. Their onboarding processes were often dull and lengthy, but they utilized users’ data to provide event recommendations.

Interview

I interviewed people with varied volunteering frequency to understand their process of looking for volunteering events and the pain points in the process. I also talked to volunteer managers in non-profit organizations to understand what’s the process like from the other side.

People often overlook the holistic goal of volunteering. Volunteering is about building a relationship with communities and is not just an academic requirement, as one of the volunteer managers pointed out.

Many infrequent volunteers said that they simply didn’t have time to volunteer. But when I dug deeper, they revealed that they found searching for volunteering opportunities to be dull and tiring. If it’s not actively presented to them, they are not going to bother looking for them.

Volunteering is all about building a relationship with the community.

Volunteering opportunities don’t actively present themselves, and it feels like ‘work’ looking for them.

Insights

Searching for Volunteer Events Feels Like “Work”

Infrequent volunteers feel the process of looking for volunteering opportunities is dull and tiring.

Infrequent Volunteers Can Be Motivated to Volunteer

Yet, Volu can't understand its users with its minimal onboarding process.

Volunteering is About Building Relationships

The holistic goal of volunteering is building a continuing relationship between volunteers and their communities.

Research Recap

Volu has insufficient knowledge of the users to provide an engaging volunteer opportunity searching experience. Also, Volu does not create strong connections between organizations, volunteers to encourage volunteering.

Proposed Changes

The improved version of the app should be able to the following three things

  1. Help users form a habit of volunteering.

  2. Gather more user information to provide better recommendations

  3. Make searching for events feel less like ‘work.’

I brainstormed different ways to achieve the goals. A more in-depth onboarding process can gather more user information and provide better event recommendations. I tested different game ideas with paper prototypes to see which one the users like the most. And for the habit-forming system, I used the concept in Hooked by Nir Eyal as the system’s foundation.

Habit-Forming System

I used Nir Eyal’s model to structure the system to help users build a habit of volunteering and establish long-term relationships with organizations.

Understanding both the internal and external triggers and rewards can encourage users to achieve the goal above.

Experience Design

I focused on creating a more in-depth yet straightforward onboarding process. The registration process is separated into several pages to avoid being daunting. I also experienced different ways to introduce and integrate the habit-forming system in the flow.

Feedback

I found that users strongly dislike creating an account just to see the volunteering events, so I rearrange the order to allow users to view the events before creating an account. Yet, they would still need to create a profile to sign up for events. Also, the event sign-up flow and profile creation flow are more specified to avoid confusion.

Some were confused about how the gamification system works. So I created introductory awards for creating a profile and registering an event. And guide them to the award page that shows users other awards and how to achieve them.

I don’t like that I have to create an account just to see the events.

How do I get an achievement? And what can I do with it?

I am not sure if I am signing up for the event of creating a profile.

Sign Up

As users invest time searching and signing up for events, the gamification system rewards the action and encourages users to create profiles.

By understanding users' skills, interests, and motivations, the app will recommend other opportunities and create personalized follow-up emails.

Daily Match

The daily match feature helps users find opportunities, making the process of looking for volunteering events seem less like work.

The user would select one card they prefer. After ten rounds, the system presents the events that match their preferences.

Gamification

Users would receive trophies for various achievements, such as:"Volunteering with the same organization on three different occasions" and "Volunteering once a month for three months in a row."

Users can add and invite their friends to compete to see who puts forth the most effort in volunteering.

What Now?

I present it to the Volu team and received generally positive feedback on my research and design, but they later pivoted to a volunteer management SaaS product for non-profits organizations.

Retrospective

First time building a habit-forming system.
This was my first time building a habit-forming system. To be honest, It was pretty rudimentary, and I am not sure if it would work as I intended. It would be great to have a more comprehensive study to understand the effectiveness of the system.

Better ways to understand the users
Having users fill out their interests manually was probably not the most efficient way to understand the users. There should be a more intelligent and subtle way to understand the users’ interests.

Ethicality of changing user’s behavior
Is it ethical to encourage users to change their behavior? Even if the end goal is beneficial, who is to determine the morality of the goal. I should’ve thought more about this before deciding to build a habit-forming system.
