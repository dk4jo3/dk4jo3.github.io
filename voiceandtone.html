Voice & Tone Guideline

How to make people write good

When fragmented communication threatened to dilute the ZDS design system’s identity, I took on a challenge beyond my comfort zone: creating a unified content framework. What started as a personal hurdle in writing evolved into an opportunity to bridge silos, foster collaboration, and deliver lasting organizational impact. This is the story of how I turned a challenge into a transformative initiative.

Act I: The Personal Challenge

A Vulnerable Start

Writing has always been a challenge for me. As someone whose first language isn’t English, I’ve often felt self-conscious about my ability to communicate clearly and effectively. But this time, the stakes felt even higher. I wasn’t just writing for myself—I was taking on a project involving multiple teams across different organizations.

Fragmented communication was creating inconsistency across the ZDS design system. Product teams worked in silos, with project managers and developers often stepping in to write content themselves. The result? A chaotic mix of tones and styles that diluted our brand voice. I’d seen similar challenges before, but this time, the scale and complexity were much larger.

While I couldn’t stop everyone from writing their own content, I realized I could provide a guiding framework to ensure consistency. This wasn’t just a gap—it was an opportunity to align the organization under a unified voice.

Getting Buy-ins

I reached beyond my core responsibilities and approached the ZDS lead, seeking resources to tackle this issue. With their support, I started with a comprehensive content audit, collaborating with stakeholders to uncover patterns of inconsistency. From there, I worked with the marketing and branding teams to define core principles, ensuring our efforts aligned with the broader organizational vision.

What about AI?

Midway through the project, a new requirement emerged: adapting for AI features within the product. These interactions needed a distinct tone, different from the standard user interface voice. While conducting an inventory of AI touchpoints, I collaborated with stakeholders to define a specific tone that acknowledged the unique role of AI in the user experience. This led to the creation of a nuanced voice chart that addressed the needs of both AI and traditional content.

Navigating Resistance

Initial resistance came from the content team, who saw this initiative as encroaching on their domain. To ease tensions, I positioned the project as a starting point—a foundation they could build upon. By offering to hand off the effort once it was established, I fostered collaboration and mutual respect.

Act III: Delivering Impact and Looking Ahead

The Deliverable: A Living Document

The culmination of this effort was a comprehensive, adaptable document that became the gold standard for content across teams. While I handed off ownership to the main ZDS team, I continued collaborating with the content team to keep it updated and relevant.

Reflections on the Process: Lessons Learned

Without a concrete deadline, maintaining momentum was a challenge. To address this, I set up regular stakeholder check-ins to ensure steady progress. I also learned the importance of flexibility—our roadmap became more of a “living plan,” adapting to new priorities and feedback.

A Fulfillment Beyond Deliverables

Despite the challenges, the project was deeply rewarding. It allowed me to create something impactful for the organization while building cross-functional relationships and expanding my own skills. The process wasn’t just about improving the product; it was about fostering collaboration and learning to navigate complex dynamics.

Looking Ahead: A Foundation for Growth

This documentation has become a foundation for consistent communication across the organization. As the company evolves, it has the potential to grow alongside it, ensuring the unified voice we’ve built continues to strengthen our brand.
