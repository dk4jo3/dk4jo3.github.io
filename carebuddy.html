Carebuddy is a system of a wearable device and an application that strive to improve the quality of life for both dementia patients and their caregivers, specifically for seniors who are cared by their family members. It utilizes technology like GPS, RFID, motion sensor, and Fitbit-like activity tracking that can be customized to actively prevent patients from harm.

Care Buddy aims to give full-time caregivers’ more free time and give patients a sense of freedom in their own homes while still receiving the same level of care from their caregivers.

Existing Products

Passive Prevention – There is no existing product that can actively prevent users from potential hazardous situation.

Fragmented Features – Current products fail to offer a complete solution and features that can accommodate all aspects of users’ daily lives.

Delayed Response – Existing products do not offer a solution to actively prevent risk from users. They can only notify the carer after the patients have already been potentially harmed.

Why do dementia patients wander?

Often, dementia patients feel overwhelmed due to overwhelming environmental factors such as loud noises and unfamiliar surroundings.

Dementia patients can sometimes go through their house searching for what they need but they sometimes end up wandering out of their residences and putting themselves in danger.

Dementia patients also can sometimes leave their residences in order to follow past daily routines in they have in their memory such as picking children up from the school or going to church.

Factors

Their typical daily routines are taken into the consideration such as hospital trips and exercising.

The device should be able to track their locations and vital signs and send alerts to their caregivers when necessary.

System Features

Alert Levels – the system categorizes different situations that would trigger alerts into three different levels of alert such as abnormal, concerning, and emergency.

Location Sensitive – The same trigger action reacts in different locations triggers and sets off different levels of alerts.

Customization – Alerts can be temporarily disable based on the activity schedule in order to accommodate different care recipient's needs.

Incremental Alerts – System would upgrade alerts if the trigger action persists.

User Interface

The interface aims not to be cognitively overwhelming for the caregivers. It uses colors to indicate care recipient’s status and large icons and text for simple navigation.

Customization

Heart Rate - The caregiver can set up safe heart rate range for the patient.

Area - The caregiver can define the hazardous areas, night areas and the distances allowed for the patient to be outside of the house.

Scheduling - The caregiver can schedule activities that could potentially falsely trigger the alarms such as exercise (accelerated heart rate) or hospital trips (leaving the residence) to prevent false alarm.

Emergency - The caregiver can determine if the system would contact emergency personnel or simply send an alert to the caregiver when it detects an emergency.
