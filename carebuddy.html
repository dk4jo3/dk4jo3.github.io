Existing Products

Passive Prevention – There is no existing product that can actively prevent users from potential hazardous situation.

Fragmented Features – Current products fail to offer a complete solution and features that can accommodate all aspects of a patient's daily life.

Delayed Response – Existing products do not offer a solution to actively prevent risk from users. They can only notify the carer after the patients possibly have already been harmed.

Why do dementia patients wander?

Often, dementia patients feel overwhelmed due to stress-inducing environmental elements such as loud noises and unfamiliar surroundings.

Dementia patients can sometimes go through their houses searching for what they need but they sometimes end up wandering out of their residences and putting themselves in danger.

Dementia patients also can sometimes leave their residences in order to perform past daily routines from their memory such as picking children up from the school or going to church.

Their typical daily routines such as hospital trips and exercising are taken into the consideration.

The device should be able to track their locations and vital signs and send alerts to their caregivers when necessary and disable certain alarm during certain activities.

This device utilizes GPS, RFID, iBeacon, heart rate monitor, and motion-sensing technology in conjunction with a companion application.

System Features

Alert Levels – the system categorizes different situations that would trigger alerts into three different levels of alert such as abnormal, concerning, and emergency.

Location Sensitive – The same trigger action in different locations can trigger different levels of alerts.

Customization – Alerts can be temporarily disable based on the activity schedule in order to accommodate different care recipient's needs.

Incremental Alerts – System would upgrade alerts if the trigger action persists.

User Interface

The interface aims not to be cognitively overwhelming for the caregivers. It uses colors to indicate care recipient’s status and large icons and text for simple navigation.

Customization

Heart Rate - The caregiver can set up safe heart rate range for the patient.

Area - The caregiver can define the hazardous areas, night areas and the distances allowed for the patient to be outside of the house.

Scheduling - The caregiver can schedule activities that could potentially falsely trigger the alarms such as exercise (accelerated heart rate) or hospital trips (leaving the residence) to prevent false alarms.

Emergency - The caregiver can determine if the system would contact emergency personnel or send an alert to the caregiver when it detects an emergency.
