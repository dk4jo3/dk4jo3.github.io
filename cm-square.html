CM² Investment Platform

A Crowd-Sourced Investment Platform Powered by Blockchain

I designed and developed CM Square Investment Platform with the focus of creating a playful yet user-centric experience that makes real-estate investing more approachable.

Context

When I joined, the platform was only an idea; I talked to the marketing department and the founders to understand the context. I wanted to find out the problems they tried to solve with this platform.

There were three main issues in the current RE investment experience:

  1. High barrier of entry: you need a considerable amount of capital even to entertain the idea of RE investing.

  2. High learning curve: the RE buying process is complicated. It’s filled with jargon and convoluted processes between multiple parties.

  3. Hard to manage: It’s challenging to manage the day-to-day RE investment by oneself or find a trustworthy RE management company.

Strategy

Based on the issues and the current stage of the start-up, I devised a strategy to guide the design.

  1. Light and playful UI: The UI should be inviting and playful to create a friendly attitude to signify the platform’s simple RE investment process.

  2. Clear and digestible data: Clear and digestible data: Since one share of the property would cost the same as a cup of coffee; we felt that we should only provide the essential data to simplify their decision-making process.

  3. Establish trust: Since the startup does not have any brand recognition, we need to convince users to trust us. On the website, We showcase the experience of team members, well-known investors, reviews, and overseas office locations to boost trustworthiness.

Purchasing Flow

We initially decided that the user would acquire CM2T tokens from other cryptocurrency exchanges to purchase the property shares. But the founders later changed to allow them to use USD if they have insufficient CM2T token to buy the property shares. Direct purchasing created a layer of complexity in the purchasing loop. Now we have to consider payment integration and make the process as smooth as possible for them.

I created flow charts to map out different scenarios and solutions. I created the wireframe and applied the current UI to it. I follow the principle mentioned in DMMT, so they don’t have to think much while going through the dialogues.
