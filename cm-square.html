CM² Investment Platform

CM Square is a cryptocurrency-based crowd-sourced real estate investment platform that enables investors to purchase as little as a single cm² of a property.

I designed and developed the CM Square Investment Platform to create a playful yet user-centric experience that makes real-estate investing more approachable. I conducted qualitative research to formulate a design strategy, used Sketch to create mockups, and developed the front-end with HTML and CSS.

CONTEXT

When I joined the team, the platform was only an idea. To better understand the problem space and context, I first talked to the marketing department and the founders to get a better sense of the problems they wanted to solve.

These were the three main issues in the current real estate investment experience:

  • High barrier of entry: you need a considerable amount of capital even to entertain the idea of real estate investing.

  • High learning curve: the real estate buying process is complicated. It’s full of technical jargon, and it’s a convoluted process between brokers, banks, and many other stakeholders.

  • Hard to manage: It’s challenging to manage the day-to-day operation of real estate investment by oneself, and it’s challenging to find a trustworthy real estate management company.

STRATEGY

Based on the issues identified and the current stage of the start-up, I devised a strategy to guide the design.

  • Light and playful UI:The UI should be inviting and playful to create a friendly attitude to signify the platform’s simple real estate investment process.

  • Clear and digestible data: Since one share of the property would cost the same as a cup of coffee, we felt that we should provide only the essential data to simplify the user’s decision-making process.

  • Establish trust: Since the startup does not have any brand recognition, we needed to showcase the experience of the team members, well-known investors, reviews, and overseas office locations on the platform to increase trustworthiness.

Multi-language Support

This platform would target customers in both Taiwan and Hong Kong. We needed to have an English and a Chinese version of the platform. I considered how English text would take up more space than the Chinese text and the subtle cultural difference between the two regions.

  • Leave enough text space for the English version of the platform

CHALLENGE

Filter design feedbacks and conflict resolution: When I received design change suggestions, I would ask what the goals were for the changes and find the best practice to achieve the goal. For instance, the marketing department wanted to put a marquee on the website to promote specific properties. So I suggested instead of a marquee, a row of cards of promoted properties would be more suitable.

Front-end limitation: Since I am also the front-end developer, I can only design what I can code. So sometimes I had to make some compromises to how the platform look. There also some more technical data visualization development that I could not do. So I had to ask the senior developer for help or abandoned it altogether.

Crypto Complexity: We had trouble figuring out how to design a crypto-based platform that’s easy to use for people unfamiliar with cryptocurrency. Moreover, it was not an option to simply hide it in the back-end because crypto-based is one of the central core values. So we decided to create an onboarding tutorial process and avoid using crypto jargon on the platform.

Purchasing Flow

We initially decided that the user would acquire CM2T token from cryptocurrency exchanges to purchase the property shares. The founders later wanted to reconsider this and allow users to use USD to buy the property shares. However, direct purchasing created a layer of complexity in the purchasing loop. Now we had to consider payment integrations and make the process as smooth as possible for users.

I created flow charts to map out different scenarios and solutions. I created wireframes that reflected the updated flow. In addition, I made sure the dialogue content was s non-technical and straightforward, so the user didn’t have to overthink while going through the dialogues.

Retrospective

Push back unreasonable design
Even though users can purchase CM2T on the platform, they still need to use cryptocurrency exchange to cash out to government-backed currency (eg, USD, HKD). I should have proposed a design plan that considers the end-to-end experience of purchasing/selling experience.

I do not want to do Front-End development for a living
Like many early-stage startups, things move extremely fast. The onboarding process was designed and developed in a week. Looking back, in addition to the onboard tutorial slideshow, I should have also created a series of tutorial videos to introduce users to the platform. Or create an interactive tutorial that guides users through the purchasing/selling process with fake token/property.

Push for more usability testings
Due to budget and time constraints, we didn’t have a chance to do in-depth user testing. However, Therefore, I would’ve pushed to have more user testings done to ensure the users understand the platform’s concept and purchase/sell shares on the platform.
