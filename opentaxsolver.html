Open Tax Solver

Open Tax Solver is an open-source tax filing software that offers users a free, and secure tax filing solution.

I redesigned the UX/UI of Open Tax Solver, an open-source, free, and secure tax filing solution, to improve its usability, appearance, and features.

UX/UI Issues

The user interface is outdated and lacks usability. Users have to manually select forms they need to file. Moreover, users are unable to file electronically via OTS. They would have to print and mail the forms.

Comp Analysis and Interview

I talked to users who use tax preparation tools like TurboTax or HR Blocks. The conversation revolved around their understanding of the tax code and their experience with their tax preparation tools, specifically about their favorite features of the tools.

Generally, they have a poor understanding of tax jargon and codes, they usually rely on the tax preparation tool to assist them. However, OTS does not really provide any assistance for the users at all.

Main Page

The main page showcases the three primary steps of filing taxes with Open Tax Solver. Users can start a new filing or load the previous filing on this page.

Questionnaire

A short questionnaire can help users determine the required tax forms for them. For more experienced tax filers, they can skip the questionnaire to select the forms directly.

Helper Dialogue

An interactive dialogue can help users with difficult parts of the forms. In this example, users can find out the dependent qualification by answering the questions in the dialogue.

Report

As Open Tax Solver does not support the e-filing service, it displays tax agencies’ addresses and tracking information. Users can save or print the finished tax forms.
