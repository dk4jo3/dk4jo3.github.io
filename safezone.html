SAFE ZONE

What will the public service be like 5 years from now?

This project explored users' perception and behavior when they are stressed or anxious. The goal of this project is to define design principles of a public space for young adults to relieve stress and learn more about mental health.

For this project, I designed and tested different research methods, and insight generation.

This project is a shared effort with Donna Greene, Krupali Patel, and Crystal Wei. A majority of the graphics were created by Donna Greene and all icons are from Project Noun.

In 2013, an estimated 43.8 million adults... in the United States had any mental illness... 10.0 million...had serious mental illness... in the past year.

— 2013 National Survey on Drug Use and Health: Mental Health Findings - SAMHSA

Actors' Map

With the initial goal of providing young adults with mental illnesses a safe space, we moved on to understand the relationship of the subject with its stakeholders by creating an actor's map, drawing connections to various actors in the subject’s life. The map helped us brainstorm what we sought to understand about the users through the survey.

Initial Questions

How do users use professional assistance and how helpful are treatments?

What are the sources of stress for them? And how do they deal with negative emotion?

From whom do they seek emotional support? And how do their friends and family view such interactions?

Survey

We tailored the questions to the general public with logic branching sections that would only appear if they answer yes if they have been medically diagnosed with a mental illness in order to understand more specifics about what it’s like to be a mental illness patient.

We received only a small sample size of 32 responses. And among them, only 4 participants admitted to being diagnosed with a mental illness. We also received feedback stating the survey was too long and too personal.

Findings

  • Distraction is used as a coping mechanism because it steers the subject away from the actual problem.
  • People feel obligated to assist their friends/family yet they often feel emotionally drained from doing so.
  • People are generally more comfortable sharing personal emotional issues with their friends than their family members.

Card Sorting

We enquired what would be a perfect relaxation space for the participants with the A.E.I.O.U method, asking them about the actors, environments, interactions, objects, and users in the space.

We selected recurring keywords and created cards with the descriptions or images corresponding to them. We then asked participants to rank them based on what matches with the perfect relaxation space they imagined from the most to the least.

Findings

  • Participants prefer a certain level of stimulation of sound, visual, and physical sensation.
  • Feeling of physical pressure like wrapping in a blanket offers them a certain level of relaxation.
  • Different kinds of stress and personality lead to different levels of preferred interaction with others.

Journey Map

We asked the participants to recall what they did yesterday and take us on a journey of their day by arranging cards with images and descriptions on the map.

Findings

  • Personal hobbies are relaxing because users are familiar with their hobbies and it usually comes with minimal responsibilities.
  • Participants envisioned the space to be near heavily-trafficked area because of the convenience
  • Familiar space is comforting because there are no surprises or sudden changes.

Co-design Plan

We showed the user our proposed design of the space then asked them what do they think the space is for? What kind of environmental elements would be in this space? Where would this space be? And have they been in a similar space before?

We then explained to them the purpose of this design and asked them to sketch on it to make this layout more suitable for its purpose from their point of view.

Findings

  • People prefer different levels of interaction depends on the source of stress and their personalities.
  • People prefer gentle natural elements because nature itself is often comforting.
  • People prefer non-threatening forms and objects.

Simulation Testing

To further validate the result of co-design sessions, we conducted a series of simulation testing on the sound, smell, and lighting of the space, using scented candles, adjustable lightings, and sounds.

Findings

  • Participants prefer natural lighting from windows or lights that mimic sunlight.
  • Participants prefer natural soft sounds like water, rain, or other subtle sounds found in nature.
  • Participants prefer natural smells like grass or rain.

Proposed Designn Principles

The aesthetics of the design should contain natural lighting, sounds, and scents. It should be comfortable, a home-away-from-home feeling

The space should be accessible 24/7 and located near a location with heavy traffic like a coffee shop, gym, or library where people can easily drop in throughout their days.

The space should have a mental health professional on staff who would only interact with the users when users initiate the interaction.

The space should enable different levels of interactions within it.
