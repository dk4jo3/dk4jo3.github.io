What will the public service will be like 5 years from now?

This project explored users' perception and behavior when they are stressed or anxious with the goal of defining main design principles of a public space designed for young adults to relief stress and learn more about their mental health.

I contributed the majority of my time designing and testing research methods, and insight generation.

This project is a shared effort with Donna Greene, Krupali Patel, and Crystal Wei, Majority of graphics were created by Donna Greene, and all icons are from Project Noun.

In 2013, an estimated 43.8 million adults... in the United States had any mental illness... 10.0 million...had serious mental illness... in the past year.

— 2013 National Survey on Drug Use and Health: Mental Health Findings - SAMHSA

Actors' Map

With the initial focus, we moved on to understand the ecological relationship of the subject within the context by creating an actors map that draws connections to various actors in the subject’s life that can possibly help us understand what we want to find out through our survey in the next step.

Initial Questions

How do the subjects utilize professional assistance, and how helpful are the treatments?

What are the sources of stress for them? And how do they deal with negative emotional energy?

From whom do they seek emotional and personal help? And how do their friends and family view such interactions?

Survey

we tailored the questions to general public with logic branching sections that would only appears if they answer yes that they have been medically diagnosed with mental illness to understand more specifically about mental illness patient.

We received only a small sample size of 32 participants, and among them only 4 admit they have been diagnosed with mental illness. We also receive feedbacks saying the survey is too long and too personal.

    Findings
  • Distraction is used as a coping mechanism, steering themselves from the real problems.
  • People feel obligated to assist their friends/family and they often feel emotionally drained doing that.
  • People are generally closer to their friends than other actors in their lives.

Card Sorting

We asked the participants to imagine their perfect relaxation space, and the activities, environments, interactions, objects, and users (A.E.I.O.U) in the space.

We selected repeating keywords and created cards with descriptions or images corresponding to them. And asked participants to rank them based on what matches their perfect relaxation space the most, to the least.

    Findings
  • Participants prefer a certain level of stimulation of sound, visual, and feel.
  • Feeling of pressure like wrapping in a blanket offers them high level of relaxation
  • Different kinds of stress lead to different levels of desired interaction with others.

Journey Map

We asked the participants to think about what they did yesterday and take us on a journey of their day by arranging cards of images and descriptions on the map.

We strive to understand more about participants’ daily routines and emotional journey, as well as where and when they might need this service the most.

    Findings
  • Personal hobbies are relaxing because one is familiar with it and it comes with minimal responsibilities.
  • People imagine the space to be located near heavily-trafficked area because they frequent there.
  • Familiar space is comforting because there’s no surprises and changes.

Co-design Plan

We showed the user our proposed layout of the space without explanation then ask them what do they think it is? What kind of environmental elements would be in this space? Where would this space be? And if they have been in a similar space before?

We then explained to them about the purpose of this space and ask them to sketch on tracing paper to make this space more suitable for its purpose fore them.

    Findings

  • People prefer different levels of interaction because of different backgrounds.
  • People prefer natural influence because natural environment is comforting.
  • People prefer non-threatening forms and objects.

Simulation Testing

To further validate the result of co-design, we conducted a series of simulation testing about the sound, smell and lighting of the space , using scented candles, adjustable lightings, and sounds.

    Findings

  • Participants prefer natural lighting from windows or that mimics sunlight
  • Participants prefer natural soft sounds like water or rain or other subtle sounds found in nature.
  • Participants prefer natural smells found in nature like grass or rain

Proposed Designn Principles

The aesthetics of the design should contain natural lighting, sounds and scents. It should be comfortable, a home-away-from-home feeling

The space should be accessible 24/7 and located near a university, coffee shop, gym or library where people can easily drop in during their daily routines

The space should provide a mental health professional who would only interact with the users when users initiate the interaction.

The space should enable different levels of interactions within it.
