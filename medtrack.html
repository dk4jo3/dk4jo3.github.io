Patients often experience long wait in the waiting room before seeing their physicians, however, the waiting rooms are rarely designed to accommodate or solve such issue.

This project began with a collaborative research endeavor with Daniel Moo, and JP Leon, then transitioned into individual ideation and final design stages.

Pre-lim Research

We researched scholarly articles, and case-studies to search for the potential solutions of improving patients’ experience in the waiting rooms and understand other difficulties they may encounter before seeing the physicians.

Competitive Analysis

I conducted the research by observing several waiting rooms in the nearby hospitals and health centers. I paid attention to the behavior of the patients, the interactions among patients, and the layout of the waiting room.

    Findings
  • Patients rarely engage with the entertainment/distraction provided by the facilities
  • The arrangement of seatings discourages social interaction among the patients.
  • Patients are mostly on their phones, laptops or other devices.

Interviews

I conducted interviews with people who have recent been in waiting rooms. I asked them about the pain points, the success of it, as well as what would they change about it.

    Findings
  • Clear indication of waiting time can reduce patients’ stress in the waiting room.
  • Patients in SU Health Center often have a hard time understanding the check-in process and receptionist are often annoyed and disrespectful in SU Health Center.

INSIGHTS

For visitors of SU Health Center, the frustration derived not only from the wait time but also from the confusing check-in process and unfriendly receptionists

Brainstorming

I combined parts of several ideas to create a web service that can streamline the process of making appointments and check-in for appointments.

Usability Testing

With multiple rounds of usability testing with paper prototypes, I refined the wireframe and visual layout by understanding the obstacles and success from the users’ perspectives.

  • Search - The search bar, and result page was changed to accommodate different keywords and search results.
  • Account - A subtle hint was added to the drop-down menu.
  • Modify- The check-in and appointment information pages were merged as one to make the information more clearly presented.
  • Color - Different colors distinguish the flows of the appointment-making and check-in process.

Experience

User can toggle between creating appointment and checking-in directly on the fixed navigation bar

The appointment making section is now a modal instead of an accordion format for the cleaner look and smoother interaction

Search and filter function is implemented to better manage the search results.
