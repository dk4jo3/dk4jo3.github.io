Patients often experience long wait in the waiting room before seeing their clinicians, however, the waiting room are rarely designed to accommodate or solve such issue. In Syracuse University Health Center, besides the wait, it is not uncommon for students to have unpleasant interaction with the receptionist, and confusing check-in process.

This project began with a collaborative research effort with Daniel Moo, and JP Leon, then transistioned into individual ideation and final design.

Pre-lim Research

We researched though scholarly articles, and case studies regarding the potential solutions to improve patients’ experience in the waiting rooms as well as understanding other difficulties they may encounter before seeing the clinicians.

Competitive Analysis

I conducted the research by observing several waiting rooms in the nearby hospitals and health centers, focusing on the behavior of the patients.

    Findings
  • Patients rarely engage with the entertainment provided by the facilities
  • The arrangement of seatings discourages interaction among the patients.
  • Patients are mostly on their phones, laptops or other mobile devices.

Interviews

I conducted interviews with people who have recent experience in the waiting rooms. I asked them about the difficulties, the success of it, as well as what would they change about it.

    Findings
  • Clear indication of waiting time can reduce patients’ stress in the waiting room.
  • Patients often have a hard time understanding the check-in process
  • Receptionist are often annoyed and disrespectful in SU Health Center.

INSIGHTS

For visitors of SU Health Center, the pain is not the wait time, but the confusing check-in process and unfriendly receptionists

Archetype
