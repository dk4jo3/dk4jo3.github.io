MEDTRACK

Patients often experience long wait times in the waiting room before seeing a physician. However, the waiting rooms are rarely designed to accommodate or solve such an issue.

This project began with a collaborative research endeavor with Danielle Mou and JP Leon, then transitioned into individual ideation and final design stages.

Pre-lim Research

We studied scholarly articles and case-studies in order to search for potential solutions of improving patients’ experience in waiting rooms and to understand the other difficulties they may encounter before seeing a physician.

Fly-on-the-wall Observation

I observed several waiting rooms in the nearby hospitals and health centers. I paid close attention to the behavior of the patients, the interactions among patients, and the layout of the waiting room.

    Findings
  • Patients rarely engage with the entertainment/distraction provided by the facilities
  • The arrangement of seatings discourages social interaction among the patients.
  • Patients are mostly on their phones, laptops or other devices.

Interviews

I conducted interviews with people who have recently been in waiting rooms. I asked them about the pain points, the successes, as well as what would they change about a waiting room.

    Findings
  • A clear indication of waiting time can reduce patients’ stress in the waiting room.
  • Patients in SU Health Center often have a hard time understanding the check-in process and receptionists are often unhelpful.

INSIGHTS

For visitors of the SU Health Center, the frustration is compounded by not only from the wait time but also from the confusing check-in process and unfriendly receptionists.

Brainstorming

I combined parts of several ideas in order to create a web service that can streamline the process of making appointments and checking-in for appointments.

Usability Testing

With multiple rounds of usability testing with paper prototypes, I refined the wireframe and visual layout by understanding the obstacles and successes from the users’ perspectives.

Search - The search bar, and result page was changed to accommodate different keywords and search results.

Account - A subtle indication was added to the drop-down menu.

Modify- The check-in and appointment information pages were merged as one to make the information more clearly presented.

Color - Different colors distinguish the respective paths of appointment-making and check-in process.

Experience Design

User can toggle between creating an appointment and checking-in directly on the fixed navigation bar. The appointment making section is now a modal instead of an accordion format for a cleaner look and smoother interaction

Search and filter function is implemented to better manage the search results.
