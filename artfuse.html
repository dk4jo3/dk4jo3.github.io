ARTFUSE

Artfuse is a social media platform that aims to connect the entire creative industry

As an UX/UI & creative strategy specialist at Artfuse, I conducted design research to validate assumptions, discovered design opportunities, and to create a minimal viable prototype to address the insights discovered, as well as uncover the market needs.

Vision

Artfuse is an entrepreneurial endeavor from a group of students at Syracuse University, led by Diethard Struelens. Artfuse is a professional social media platform that aims to connect the entirety of the creative industry, ranging from writers to performing artists, as well as other significant stakeholders in their professional lives.

Competitive Analysis

From analyzing existing social media platforms, it is evident that there are significant gaps between art, design, and performance art social media platforms. In the current creative industry, interdisciplinary collaboration is a huge part of it yet it is often overlooked by existing platforms.

Survey

Questions on a survey I fielded investigated participants’ behaviors and perception of art/design social media, as well as the way participants view their own work.

From the 44 samples gathered, the majority of them disagree that existing art/design social media platforms are helpful in professional development and connecting with other creative disciplines. The majority of them agree that they lack certain business and management skills that are essential in professional settings.

More than half of the responses stated besides having presences on social media platforms, they also have personal websites.

Literature Review

I interviewed a wide range of interviewees, from a professional photographer whose works have been exhibited in the MET and Getty Museum to a recently-graduated performance artist who currently resides in Los Angeles. Some similarities they share are the dissatisfaction over the lack of transparent monetary transaction in the current industry and the exhaustive yet empowering business-related process of selling their artworks, such as setting up exhibitions, networking with potential buyers, or actively searching for audition opportunities.

Insights

Lack of monetary transparency in the transaction process
Artists/Designer rarely have a clear understanding of how much the gallery or agents are gaining from their work or performances.

Young and emerging creatives often lack certain essential professional skills
The majority of participants responded saying that they do not have any substantive legal or business knowledge for the professional environment.

Work-flow is deeply fragmented throughout multiple tools and platforms
Artists and designer have to go through variety of tools and platforms to complete one project. The communication channel is often complex with the mix of digital and physical mediums.

Proposed Solution

Artfuse can be the central hub for the artist/designers’ online professional interactions. It can be like LinkedIn but designed specifically for creative professionals. They can connect with others, start a project, and communicate with clients and others all in one place.

Users would be able to customize their profiles and workflows to accommodate their personal preferences and project needs.

Project management tools can help creative professionals along with the process by providing contract or timeline templates. The workflow of it can also be customized to suit different types of projects.

Target Audience : Young and emerging artist/designers who would benefit from connecting with other creative professionals, assistance for professional development, and hands-on project management.

Experience

Started with rough sketches, I continuously tested the prototype with users as I became more aware of the pain points and successes of the experience.

The process of starting a project in the project dashboard tool is streamlined so the user would not have to go back to the main page to do so.

To accommodate for users with vastly different needs, the main portal is implemented in order to lead them to the contents that are the most useful and interesting to them.

The flow of discovering different artists and designers from the profile page is improved so the users would have a direct path to discover artists instead of needing to go to back the front page to do so.

Interface

The size differences of cards leave potential space for future monetization strategies. The users can potentially pay to promote his/her works on the front page in a larger format.

Different ways to navigate through the platform with the minimal number of clicks needed are tested and implemented.

The complex information is distilled and broken down into digestible bits with the option to view more details with hover.

Front Page

Display projects with differentiated hierarchy and visual emphasis

The page display features the most popular or most liked works in the different categories with the ability to filter the content

Only images are shown to maintain a clear interface but users can hover to view other images in the project, as well as the likes, views, and the information of the creator

The modal provides more detailed information about the project, as well as comments, larger images, creator’s information, and a link to message the creator.

Profile

Ability to customize a personal page to promote projects and showcase unique individuality.

This feature further eliminates the need for personal websites and enable users to customize their pages extensively, which is not offered by other art/design social media platforms.

The size of project tiles can be customized. Artfuse supports file formats such as gif, video, or text on to accommodate different type of works

Project Dashboard

Communication, contract templates, updates, and other tools are all in one place to help users manage their projects.

To assist young and emerging creators, the project management tool can be customized to suit different types of projects yet offer the users a clear and logical workflow they can follow.

The dashboard is able to embed tools such as Google Doc, Google drive, and PDF viewer to keep all the documents in one place and congregate the communication channels between the different parties.

Creators can post updates of the project within the dashboard and share with the clients to ensure a clear and close communication channel.

Responsive

As the platform has not yet prepared to launch its mobile application, the current website is designed to be responsive based off of a Bootstrap framework in order to accommodate mobile and tablet users.
