Vision

A group of students at Syracuse University, led by Diethard Struelens, founded Artfuse, a professional social media platform for the creative industry.

HYPOTHESIS

There is a need for creator/artist social media networks. Existing platforms such as Linkedin, Behance, Dribble, and others do not satisfy their collaboration, professional development, and client outreach needs.

Design Research

Competitive Analysis

There are significant gaps between art, design, and performance art social media platforms.

Survey Says...

Most performers, artists, and designers express that they lack the essential business and project management knowledge. More than half state they have both personal websites and social media presences.

Interviews

They are dissatisfied with the creative industry’s untransparent financial information and have a love-hate relationship with the exhaustive yet empowering business-related processes.

View more on the research process  

Insights

Lack of Transparency in the Works’ Values

Artists and designers rarely have a clear understanding of the profit galleries and agents gain from their works.

Lack of Professional Skills

Emerging artists and designers often lack essential professional skills. They do not have the legal, business, and project management knowledge for the professional environment.

Workflow is Deeply Fragmented

They need to use multiple platforms and communication channels for a single project.

RESEARCH RECAP

It is partially valid that creators/artists are unsatisfied with existing social media platforms: They need assistance on the freelance process’s legal and business aspects. Moreover, their freelance workflow is spread throughout multiple platforms, often causing miscommunication and unmet expectations.

Proposed Solution

Artfuse can be the central hub for artists’ and designers’ professional online interactions. It will be a social media platform designed specifically for creative professionals. Users can connect, collaborate, communicate with other stakeholders all in one place.

Users can customize their profiles and workflows to accommodate their needs. Besides, project management tools guide them through the freelance process.

Target Audience : Artists and designers who need assistance for professional development and hands-on project management tools.

Experience Design

I tested the prototype to improve its usability. The main portal leads users of different creative disciplines to the contents that are the most useful to them.

I simplified the flow of discovering other artists and designers so users can quickly find new artists on the main page.

Interface Design

The size hierarchy of cards on the front page leaves space for future monetization strategies. Users can pay to promote their works on the front page.

I broke down complex information into digestible bits. Users can view more details by hovering over each card.

Front Page

Users can display projects with different levels of emphasis.

The page features the most popular and most liked works in different categories.

Users can hover over each project card to see the number of likes, views, and creators’ information.

The modal provides detailed project information: comments, larger images, the creator’s information, and a button to message the creator.

Profile

Users can customize their pages to promote projects and showcase individuality.

This feature eliminates the need for personal websites and enables users to customize their pages extensively, which other art/design social media platforms don’t offer.

Users can customize the size of the project card. Artfuse supports file formats such as gif, video, and text to accommodate different types of content.

Project Dashboard

Communications, contract templates, updates, and other tools are all in one place to help users manage their projects.

The project management tool can be customized to suit different projects and offer users a clear and logical workflow.

The dashboard supports tools like Google Docs, Google Drive, and PDF viewer to keep all the documents in one place and consolidate communication channels.

Responsive

As the platform has not yet prepared to launch its mobile application, the current website is responsive to accommodate mobile and tablet users.

User Interface V2

Pivot

We decided to simplify the functions and reshape the focus of the platform. Considering the current pandemic, we found the best way to help independent artists connect them with potential customers and offer them ways to perform virtually.

Artfuse Experience

Target Customer: Music lovers who want to enjoy live music by independent artists during the pandemic.

Users can buy tickets to join virtual Zoom concerts, book private virtual performances, and send personalized performance videos to others.

They simply need to select the type of service they need and fill out a short form. Based on the information provided, Artfuse will connect them to the right artists.
