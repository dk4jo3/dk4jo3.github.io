ARTFUSE

Artfuse is a professional social media platform for artists and designers.

As a UX/UI and creative strategy specialist at Artfuse. I conducted design research to narrow down the target user group, validate hypotheses, and uncover design opportunities. I also developed mockups and interactive prototypes for the platform.

Vision

When I joined Artfuse, the founder had already assembled a small team of marketing, IT, and finance students. They had a general idea of what they want to build and who the target users would be.

They wanted to create a professional social media platform for artists and designers to satisfy their collaboration, professional development, and client outreach needs. They believed there was a market gap for the platform.

I began by creating a research plan to validate their hypothesis, uncover design opportunities and narrow down the target user group.

Hypothesis

Existing platforms such as Linkedin, Behance, Dribble, and others do not satisfy their collaboration, professional development, and client outreach needs. And there is a need for a professional social media network for artists/designers that provides those services.

Proposed Solution

We have narrowed down our target user groups to emerging freelance artists and designers who would benefit from professional development and project management assistance.

We have bounced around different ideas on how to translate the research insights into design. We thought about building

  • An educational platform for emerging freelance artists
  • A freelance project management tool
  • A talent marketplace
  • A freelance job matching platform.

But the team loved the idea of the social media platform for artists/designers with a project management feature. We felt this is a great starting point, and we can easily add features later on without pigeon-holing ourselves into a single specialized product.

We envisioned Artfuse as the central hub for artists/designersâ€™ professional online interactions, similar to LinkedIn but designed specifically for artists/designers. Users can connect, collaborate, communicate with other stakeholders all in one place. Users can customize their profile pages to showcase their individualities and utilize the project management tool to navigate the freelance process. It can

  • Cut out the middleman and empower artists and designers.
  • Provide assistance to kickstart oneâ€™s freelance journey.
  • Consolidated communications and tools to help artists and designers manage their freelance projects.

Experience Design

I began mapping out the structure of the platform, focusing on the four main pages:

  1. Project Dashboard - where users can manage their projects all in one place
  2. Profile Page - where users can customize their profile page to showcase past projects and individuality
  3. Job Page - where users can find potential freelance opportunities,
  4. Newsfeed Page - users can see the art/design from other artists/design and interact with them.

We decided to emphasize artists'/designersâ€™ works and keep the front page clean for the interface. It also leaves space for future monetization strategy; Users or companies can pay to have their works or ads take up a larger area on the front page.

Improvement

At this stage, we were ready to take this project to startup competitions and incubators. We had a series of discussions with our mentors and users to understand how we can improve the design and the business to be more attractive to the investors.

You should also understand how other stakeholders like galleries, booking agents, or labels can utilize this platform.

We decided to focus on a couple of things

  • Improved the aesthetic of the platform. Users found the prettier UI more usable, and investors/startup judges are drawn to fleshed out UI.
  • Abandoned variable tile-format and customizable profile page to ensure we provide a consistent experience across the platform
  • Expand the potential features and tools that can empower artists and designers.

We realized the customizable profile page could not truly replace the creatorâ€™s need for a personal page, no matter how extensive the customization is. We abandoned the profile customization feature and the variable-size til format and opted for a uniform card system to ensure a consistent experience throughout the platform.

Also, we decided to focus on how we can further empower the artists/designers and developed different tools/features that can help them market their works, reach out to clients, and understand the legal/business aspect of the freelance process.

Retrospective

Know your audience.
When I presented my design, I assumed that as long as the design is user-focused backed with research, I would be able to persuade my team or panels of judges. But I forgot to consider the audience of my presentation. I failed to see from their perspective and to consider building an engaging narrative or including important metrics to back my design.

Keep users in mind throughout different stages of design.
Sometimes, the team came up with a seemingly innovative feature and got too excited to think if this is something the users would need. It would be much later that I remember to validate if this feature can benefit them.

Test early and often
I wish we had more time to validate the solution early on. We understood the problem from the user research, but we didnâ€™t know if our solution was the right one. We tested for usability, but we didnâ€™t test to see if our solution is correct.
